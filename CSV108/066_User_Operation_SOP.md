## 1. Purpose and scope

This SOP defines routine regulated use of QCLabOne 2.0 by analysts, reviewers, supervisors and QA. Users shall perform only activities authorised by role and training.

## 2. Prerequisites

- Active individual corporate account and approved application role.
- Completed role-based training and relevant laboratory qualification.
- Access to approved analytical methods and local safety procedures.
- No credential sharing, unattended signed-in session or use of another person’s account.

## 3. Sample receipt and custody

1. Open the pending SAP request and verify material, batch, sample type and quantity.
2. Generate/print the QCLabOne sample label; verify readable Sample ID/barcode.
3. Record actual receipt date/time, condition, quantity, unit and location.
4. Reject or place on hold any damaged, mismatched or insufficient sample; record reason and notify supervisor.
5. Record every aliquot, transfer, return and disposal event contemporaneously.

## 4. Test execution

1. Confirm the assigned test, effective specification/method and own qualification.
2. Start the approved LES; do not use an obsolete printed worksheet as the official record.
3. Select valid reagent, standard and instrument records.
4. Use direct instrument/CDS capture where configured.
5. For permitted manual entry, verify unit/context; provide reason and independent verification where required.
6. Never overwrite or conceal an original result. Use the controlled correction, invalidation or repeat workflow.
7. Electronically submit only after reviewing completeness and source references.

## 5. OOS/OOT and exceptions

Do not average away, delete or replace an unexpected result. Place the test/sample on hold, retain all original data, create/link the eQMS case and follow the OOS/OOT SOP. Interface/instrument errors remain in the exception queue and are reprocessed only by authorised personnel.

## 6. Review and approval

The reviewer confirms identity, method/specification version, source data/link, calculation, units, limits, exceptions, repeat rationale, relevant audit trail and signature history. The reviewer records the review decision and electronic signature. Self-approval is prohibited.

## 7. Corrections

Use the application correction function. Enter an accurate reason; do not use vague text such as “error.” A critical post-signature correction invalidates affected approval and must be re-reviewed.

## 8. Reports and retrieval

Generate only approved report templates for GMP use. Confirm record ID, template version, pagination and signatures. Ad hoc extracts require authorised access and preserved query criteria.

## 9. Outage operation

Use only issued pre-numbered contingency forms. Record source, time, user and verifier. After restoration, enter/reconcile each item once, link the form ID and obtain independent reconciliation approval.

## 10. Data-integrity prohibitions

Prohibited actions include shared accounts, backdating, trial entries in production, deleting/replacing original data, uncontrolled local copies, bypassing workflow, using unapproved calculations or asking an administrator/vendor to change production data without approved process.

## 11. Records

Sample/test records, signatures, audit trail, OOS links, reports and contingency reconciliations are retained electronically according to the retention SOP.
